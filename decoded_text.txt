Abstract: In this paper, we proposed the improved
iterative closest point algorithm based on laser radar and
IMU. The system composition is described. The initial
pose estimates of two scanning point clouds are obtained
by IMU, and extraction of line feature of two 2-D point
sets is put forward to accomplish the matching of fast
changing scenario. The experiment is carried out, and
the results show that the improved ICP algorithm could
accelerate the iteration of ICP and improve the
performance of point clouds.
Keywords: Laser radar; SLAM; Iterative closest points;
Pose estimate
1 Introduction
Indoor mobile robot (IMR), as a kind of automatic
intelligent equipment, has attracted more and more
attention. With the increase of human cost and the
improvement of automation and intelligence in the
production process, demand for IMR will be more
urgent for enterprise. Practically, laser navigation, as a
crucial method to realize simultaneous localization and
mapping (SLAM), based on laser radar has been widely
studied and successfully applied in IMR [1-3]. The
problem of SLAM is more closely related to
environmental representation, map building, location
technology, path planning and motion control.
The SLAM technology is mostly divided into two
methods, which are the probability estimation method
and the non probability estimation method. The former
applies probability theory to the positioning process of
mobile robot. The typical methods are ICP SLAM [2, 4],
extended Kalman filter (EKF) SLAM [5, 6] and particle
filter (PF) SLAM [7]. The latter is Graph SLAM [8].
Presently, the time costs of EKF-SLAM, PF-SLAM and
Graph-SLAM are enormous on account of the complex
operation process. Especially, in [5, 6], the intelligent
optimization methods are incorporated into the SLAM
algorithm. It is difficult to realize these algorithms in
practical. On contrary, the ICP-SLAM is realized only
using the matching of two frames point clouds, which
was verified in past years. However, the traditional ICP
method needs to be improved because of the lack of
initial pose estimate and effective matching.
In this paper, the improved iterative closest points based
on laser radar and IMU is proposed. In Section 2, the
IMR system composition is described. Section 3
improved the ICP method by initial pose estimate and
extraction of line feature. In Section 4, the real
experiment is carried out to verify the improved ICP
method. Finally, some conclusions are achieved.
2 The system composition
The block diagram of hardware composition for laser
navigation IMR system is shown in Figure 1. The sensor
system is divided into internal and external sensors. The
external sensor is two-dimensional laser radar, which
could perceive environment and obtain the distance and
angle information. The external sensors consist of two
encoders, IMU and an electronic compass. Two encoders
are mounted on the driving wheel of IMR to estimate the
pose. Although it is inevitable for drift of IMU integral
measurement and effect of magnetic field for electronic
compass, the fusion algorithm could be adapted to make
up this defect and improve the accuracy of position
measurement.

navigation IMR system

We use the singular value decomposition (SVD) to
obtain the optimal estimate. It is significant to select the
initial rotation matrix R and pose vector T [9]. Thus, we
achieve the initial data through inertial measurement
unit (IMU). The Improved ICP scan matching is shown
in Figure 2. The whole scheme is divided into two parts,
and the process is as follows:
1. The IMU and compass are used to acquire the initial
pose estimate.
2. The numbers of 2-D point sets are compared to judge
fast changing scenario.
3. If the scenario changes fast, the local straight line is
extracted from the original laser radar data. The initial
pose estimate and local straight line are applied to ICP
matching to improve the accuracy of matching.
4. Contrarily, the only initial pose estimate is adopted to
improve the ICP matching.

5 Conclusions
The improved ICP algorithm based on laser radar and
IMU is proposed. Some conclusions can be achieved: (1)
compared with traditional ICP, the improved ICP has
converged after third iterations. The convergence speed
is accelerated. (2) The accuracy of ICP matching is
assured by fusion of IMU data and line feature of laser
radar data.
